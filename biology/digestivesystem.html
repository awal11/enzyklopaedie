The Digestive System

The digestive system is responsible for the digestion of food and the extraction of nutrients, so that the body can later use them. Digestive system has two types of organs: the organs connected to the digestive tract (i.e. stomach, esophagus, etc.) and the subsidary organs of the digestion (i.e. liver, gall bladder, etc.). While the organs that belong to the digestive tract are directly involved with the processing of food, i.e. the nutrition goes through them, the subsidary organs only help digesting the food by producing substances. These substances chemically decompose the food.

Directly involved organs Mouth Food pipe (Esophagus) Stomach Small intestine Large intestine
Indirectly involved organs Liver Gall bladder Pancreas
